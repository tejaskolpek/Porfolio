Project Asvins - Patient Healthcare Managment system

Project Asvins is a one-stop web platform hub for all things healthcare. The goal of Project Asvins is to organize resources for patients, doctors, and insurance agencies so that they can access everything they need right in one place. Patients will be able to find doctors that fit their needs and contact them right through their browser, they will also be able to manage their insurance and easily see to do tasks for upcoming appointments. Doctors will be able to access their patient's medical history and give feedback. Insurance agencies will be able to view their patient’s information and communicate deals and information to patients.

Employee Churn Prediction

Retaining current employees is more difficult for the HR team than recruiting new ones. Any business that loses one of its valuable employees suffers a loss in terms of productivity, time, money, and other factors. This loss could be reduced if HR could able to foresee future employees who were considering leaving their positions; as a result, we looked into ways to address the employee turnover issue from a machine learning perspective through this project. When the time comes to lay off workers as part of organizational changes, the corporation can use churn modeling to make a rational decision rather than randomly selecting layoff candidates.

Skills learned and applied: Data collection, Data Mining, Data Pre Processing, Python, Pandas, Exploratory Data Analysis, Dimensionality Reduction, Machine Learning algorithms, Tuning ML model performance

We created a device which classifies Air Quality in real-time. The heart of this endeavor lay in the comprehensive dataset we curated. Data collection spanned diverse environments, capturing a spectrum of air quality conditions. This step was pivotal in ensuring the accuracy and robustness of the subsequent machine learning phase. With a well-prepared dataset in hand, we ventured into the realm of machine learning. The objective was to create a system capable of not only monitoring air quality but also providing accurate classifications when faced with new and untested environmental data inputs. The results were indeed remarkable. The system consistently achieved a classification accuracy exceeding 90% when confronted with previously unseen environmental data.
